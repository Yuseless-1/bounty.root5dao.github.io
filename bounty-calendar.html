Campaign overview
Structure

The ROOT5 DAO bounty program runs for approximately 90 days. Competitions and clipping cover the first 8 weeks, staking runs for 12 weeks, and final distributions close out the campaign.

  • Days 1–56: Competitions & clipping campaign active
  • Days 1–84: Staking active
  • Days 85–90: Final distributions & wrap‑up
See more ▾

Weekly cycles are used to keep things predictable: competitions and clipping follow a Monday → Sunday structure with distributions early in the week, while staking distributions are designed to fall later in the week.

  • Competition & clipping weeks: Monday → Sunday
  • Competition/clipping distributions: typically Mondays
  • Staking distributions: typically Fridays

Exact timestamps and transaction hashes will be posted in the ROOT5 Telegram and mirrored via the bot so participants can verify each distribution on‑chain.

Month 1 is primarily about onboarding, discovery, and establishing habits: joining channels, learning the flow, and proving you’re active.

  • Best time to solidify Tier 2 and begin moving toward Tier 3.
  • Ideal window to get comfortable with clipping and raid schedules.
  • Staking from Day 1 rewards early conviction.
Months 2 & 3 — Peak activity & wrap‑up
Weeks 5–12

Month 2 continues competitions and clipping (Weeks 5–8). Month 3 is focused on staking and finalizing tiers (Weeks 9–12), followed by a final distribution window.

  • Weeks 5–8: Final phase of competitions & clipping
  • Weeks 9–12: Staking only, no new competitions or clips
  • Week 13: Final distributions and leaderboard publish
See more ▾

Month 2 (Weeks 5–8)

  • Same weekly cycle as Month 1: Monday → Sunday.
  • Competitions and clipping end on Day 56 (end of Week 8).
  • Final competition and clipping distributions occur at the start of Week 9.

Month 3 (Weeks 9–12)

  • No new competitions or clipping rewards.
  • Staking continues and pays out weekly (typically Fridays).
  • Tier adjustments may be finalized based on overall contribution.

Final week (Week 13)

  • Final staking distribution.
  • Tier 1–3 distributions are executed.
  • Competition and clipping leaderboards are published.
  • Post‑mortem and roadmap discussion for next phase.

Weekly rhythm

  • Monday: New week starts; previous week’s comps/clips distributed.
  • Midweek: Raids, discussion pushes, creator output.
  • Weekend: Final engagement and submissions for that week.
  • Friday (staking): Staking distributions (for Weeks 1–12).

Best practice

  • Set personal reminders for raid times and clipping deadlines.
  • Track your own contributions to verify against bot tracking.
  • Check Telegram regularly around distribution days for updates and links.

Projected vs. actual

  • This calendar is a projected schedule.
  • Exact timestamps and on‑chain links will always be announced in Telegram.
  • If the DAO votes to adjust timing, this calendar can shift slightly.

Always treat on‑chain transactions and official announcements as the ultimate source of truth.
